Why was the data set created?

Purpose

  • Compare temporal and spatial trends in internet access across the United States.
  • Identify areas with relatively high representation of people with possible internet access barriers. These people may be vulnerable during evacuations and environmental emergencies.
  • Support prevention, planning, and outreach strategies to communicate with these at-risk groups before an environmental emergency occurs.
  • These data can also be used to identify proliferation of broadband internet access.

How was the data set created?

Derivation of measure

These measures were generated using the American Community Survey (ACS) 5-year estimates data from the U.S. Census Bureau.

What are the limitations of the data set?

The American Community Survey (ACS) data are used to estimate characteristic distributions. It is also important to keep in mind that all ACS data are estimates. Data are collected from a sample of the population in the United States and Puerto Rico rather than from the whole population. To help interpret the reliability of the estimates, the Census Bureau publishes a margin of error (MOE) for every ACS estimate. Guidance for Data Users of ACS can be found: https://www.census.gov/programs-surveys/acs/guidance.html.

Manmade geographic boundaries and their names are not static. Substantial changes to county or county equivalent entities that affect these data during a particular decade may be found on the Census website at https://www.census.gov/geo/reference/county-changes.html. Data comparisons in these areas should be conducted with these changes in mind.

How should these data be used?

Use Constraints

ACS data are based on survey responses. The computer and internet use questions were added to the ACS in 2013 and were mandated by the 2008 Broadband Improvement Act. The questions follow a "yes" or "no" format and respondents could select more than one category if applicable. These questions are not asked for the group quarters population, so do not include data about people living in housing such as dorms, prisons, nursing homes, etc. Guidance specific to these measures can be found here: https://www2.census.gov/programs-surveys/acs/tech_docs/subject_definitions/2018_ACSSubjectDefinitions.pdf?#. Guidance and information on data quality and limitations is available on the Census ACS site at http://www.census.gov/programs-surveys/acs/guidance.html.
